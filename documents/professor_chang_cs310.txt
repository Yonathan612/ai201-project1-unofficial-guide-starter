Professor Chang - CS 310: Database Systems

Students describe Professor Chang as practical and project-focused. Many reviews say the class is useful because students build real SQL queries and learn database design through hands-on assignments.

The workload is moderate. Weekly labs involve SQL, normalization, ER diagrams, joins, and small database design tasks. Students say the labs are manageable when they follow the examples from lecture.

Exams are fair and usually match the topics emphasized in class. Students mention that understanding joins, keys, indexes, and normalization is more important than memorizing definitions. Review sheets are helpful.

Professor Chang gives detailed feedback on database design projects. Students like that she explains why a schema is flawed instead of only marking it wrong. Some students say grading can be strict, especially on normalization mistakes.

Best advice from students: practice SQL regularly, review old labs before exams, and pay close attention to ER diagrams.
