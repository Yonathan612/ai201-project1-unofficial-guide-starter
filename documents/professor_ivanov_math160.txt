Professor Ivanov - MATH 160: Calculus I

Students describe Professor Ivanov as traditional, direct, and very focused on practice. The course covers limits, derivatives, applications of derivatives, and basic integrals.

The workload is moderate. Homework is frequent and mostly computational. Students who practice problems regularly say the class is manageable, while students who fall behind say it becomes difficult quickly.

Exams are based on lecture examples, homework, and practice problems. Students say exams are fair but timed tightly. Algebra mistakes are a common reason students lose points.

Feedback is limited on homework, but Professor Ivanov explains common exam mistakes in class. Students say office hours are useful if they bring specific problems.

Best advice from students: practice derivatives every week, review algebra basics, and do not wait until the exam week to study.
