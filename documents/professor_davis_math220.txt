Professor Davis - MATH 220: Linear Algebra

Students say Professor Davis is clear, structured, and very lecture-based. The class focuses on matrices, systems of equations, vector spaces, eigenvalues, and proofs.

The workload is moderate to heavy depending on math background. Homework is assigned almost every week and can be time-consuming because problems require multiple steps. Students say the textbook is useful for extra examples.

Exams are heavily based on homework and examples shown in lecture. Students who redo homework problems before exams usually perform better. Some students say the exams are long but fair.

Feedback is average. Professor Davis usually marks mistakes clearly but does not always provide long written explanations. Students say office hours are helpful for understanding proof-based questions.

Best advice from students: do every homework problem, practice row reduction until it feels automatic, and ask questions early when proofs become confusing.
